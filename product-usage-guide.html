Compression Therapy

Normatec
Compression Boots

What It Does

Normatec's pneumatic compression technology uses dynamic air pressure to mimic the natural muscle pump of your legs. Inflating chamber by chamber from the foot upward, the boots systematically flush metabolic waste — including lactic acid and inflammatory byproducts — out of tired muscles while simultaneously pumping fresh, oxygenated blood back in.

This is the same technology trusted by Olympic teams, NFL franchises, NBA squads, and elite endurance athletes around the world. What used to require a team physio can now happen in your hotel room while you relax.

Key Benefits

Reduces DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness) — less stiffness the morning after a hard training session, race, or long travel day.

Clears lactic acid buildup — accelerates the body's natural recovery process by hours, not just minutes.

Enhances lymphatic drainage — reduces swelling and inflammation in the lower limbs.

Improves circulation — great for long-haul travelers, people on their feet all day, and anyone prone to swelling.

Trusted by professionals — used by NFL, NBA, marathoners, and Olympic programs worldwide.

How to Use
1
Set up on the bed or a flat surface. Lay the boot sleeves flat and connect the air hose to the control unit.
2
Slide your legs in. Sit comfortably with legs inserted up to the hip. You should be relaxed — lying back or reclining is ideal.
3
Power on and select your intensity. Start at a lower setting and increase to your comfort level. The device will run through automated cycles.
4
Relax for 30–60 minutes. The boots do all the work. Use this time to read, watch TV, or pair with the red light therapy panel.
5
Power off and remove. You should feel noticeably lighter in your legs. Drink water to support continued flush of metabolic waste.

Key Benefits

Percussive Therapy

Hyperice
Percussion Gun

What It Does

The Hyperice Hypervolt delivers rapid, high-frequency percussive vibrations directly into muscle tissue, providing deep tissue therapy that a foam roller simply cannot replicate. The percussive action breaks up adhesions and knots, activates the nervous system, increases blood flow to targeted areas, and significantly improves range of motion in minutes.

Equally effective as a warm-up tool to activate and prime muscles before exercise, or as a recovery tool to release tightness and speed up post-workout repair. Trusted in the training rooms of major sports franchises and Olympic programs worldwide.

Key Benefits

Releases tight muscles and knots — targets specific areas of tension for immediate relief from tightness or cramping.

Breaks up adhesions and scar tissue — improves mobility in problem areas through consistent use.

Increases range of motion — pre-performance warm-up in 2 minutes that outperforms static stretching alone.

Improves local circulation — increased blood flow to treated areas accelerates healing and reduces DOMS.

Whisper-quiet motor — won't disturb neighbors. Use comfortably any time of night.

How to Use
1
Select the right attachment head. Ball head for large muscles (quads, glutes, back). Bullet head for pinpoint knots. Flat head for general use. Fork head for spine/Achilles.
2
Start on a lower speed. Especially for first-time use. Lower speeds are more relaxing; higher speeds are more intense and stimulating.
3
Glide slowly over the muscle. Don't hold on one spot for more than 15–30 seconds. Move across the entire muscle belly in slow passes.
4
For knots: Hold on the tight spot at low pressure until you feel it release (30–60 seconds). Don't force it — let the vibration do the work.
5
Avoid bony areas, joints, and the spine directly. Stick to muscle tissue. 1–2 minutes per muscle group is plenty for most sessions.

Cold mode constricts blood vessels to immediately reduce puffiness, dark circles, and inflammation. Heat mode relaxes facial muscles, opens pores, promotes lymphatic drainage, and significantly improves skin elasticity. Clinical results: 89% of participants reported a more rejuvenated eye area after a single use.

Key Benefits

Instantly reduces under-eye puffiness — cold constricts blood vessels and drains pooled fluid from beneath the eyes in minutes.

Diminishes dark circles — improved circulation and reduced congestion visibly brightens the under-eye area.

Promotes lymphatic drainage — heat mode stimulates the lymphatic system, reducing facial bloat and puffiness from travel, alcohol, or poor sleep.

Improves skin elasticity — regular heat use firms and tones facial skin over time.

89% single-use results — clinically proven to enhance radiance and eye area appearance after just one session.

Work down the jawline and neck for full lymphatic benefit. Long strokes from jaw to collarbone help move fluid out of the face.
5
3–5 minutes total is enough. The wand charges via USB-C and includes a travel cap for protection. Store in the kit bag between sessions.

Cold
Plunge

The ultimate recovery upgrade. Cold water immersion triggers one of the most powerful physiological recovery responses the body can experience. Available exclusively as part of the Full Recovery Suite — our premium upgrade package that includes everything in the Standard Kit plus the Cold Plunge, delivered and set up directly in your room by our team.

What It Does

Cold water immersion at temperatures between 45°F–59°F (7°C–15°C) creates an immediate and powerful systemic response. Blood vessels constrict, forcing blood from the extremities toward the core — and then vasodilate when you exit the plunge, creating a powerful circulation flush throughout the entire body.

Simultaneously, the cold triggers a massive release of norepinephrine — up to 300% increase — dramatically reducing systemic inflammation, boosting mood, sharpening mental clarity, and releasing the kind of natural endorphin rush that leaves you feeling sharp, energized, and genuinely alive.

Key Benefits

Dramatically reduces muscle inflammation & soreness — the most rapid and effective acute inflammation reduction available without medication.

Boosts circulation and lymphatic drainage — the vasoconstriction/vasodilation cycle is nature's most powerful circulatory flush.

Improves mental clarity and mood — norepinephrine release clears brain fog and elevates mood for hours post-plunge.

Releases natural endorphins — the post-plunge feeling of calm energy and euphoria is a well-documented physiological response.

Increases stress resilience — regular cold exposure builds the parasympathetic nervous system's ability to regulate stress response.

How to Use
1
Our team sets it up in your room. You don't need to do anything. It arrives ready to use at your preferred temperature.
2
Start at 55–59°F if you're new to cold plunging. More experienced users can go 45–50°F. The cold is the point — don't turn it up.
3
Control your breathing first. Before entering, take 3–5 slow, deep diaphragmatic breaths. This primes your nervous system and prevents the gasp response.
4
Immerse to the shoulders if possible. Start with 2–3 minutes for beginners. Build toward 10–15 minutes over multiple sessions. Focus on slow, controlled breathing throughout.
5
Do NOT immediately warm up with a hot shower. Let the body warm naturally for 5–10 minutes first — this is when the norepinephrine and endorphin release peaks.
